Biographies

 

Andrea Brady

Andrea Brady was born in Philadelphia, USA, in 1974, and studied at Columbia University and then at Cambridge, where she wrote a PhD on seventeenth-century poetry. She teaches early modern literature as well as contemporary British and American poetry at Brunel University. Books of poetry include Embrace (Object Permanence, 2005) and Vacation of a Lifetime (Salt, 2001). With Keston Sutherland she runs the small press Barque (www.barquepress.com). Andrea is the Director of the ‘Archive of the Now’, an online repository of innovative British poetry: www.archiveofthenow.com.

Rosheen Brennan

Rosheen Brennan is studying for the MA in Poetic Practice at Royal Holloway, University of London. Her recent work was featured in How2's previous issue, (see 'London Calling' http://www.asu.edu/pipercwcenter/how2journal/current/london/index.html)

She is interested in exploring writing concerned with the present and its unintelligibilities, especially in relation to modernist poetics and philosophy. She recently read at Openned in London.
