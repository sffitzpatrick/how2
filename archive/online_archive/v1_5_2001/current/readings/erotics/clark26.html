THANKS

And now under thanks, which are a kind of praise, and a kind of love but no kind of abstraction, I want to acknowledge that where writing is that which “appears when there's no one there to hear,” thanks are maybe alike distensions of the individual.

But rhetoric’s eroticized violence means “we” are its faith [static] as Levinas' “intimacy” and “austerity” – what Simone Weil might have called the “personal” and the “impersonal” relation, which she saw operating together in saints –in the tender encounter between I and the Other who creates me is vs. the giddy, wastrel, outrageous waste of love's sweetness into the sucking void of adoration – what use?

Thought (language) in which tasking is impossible, is a form of ardent belief. This kind of praise reorganizes language. And how far am I from thanking any syllogism I could member short of aporia for your body in my arms?

 

 

bio: Susan Clark edits Raddle Moon with contributing editors Nicole Brossard, Norma Cole, Erin Mouré and Lisa Robertson, and scout, Catriona Strang; and is responsible for the lapse of a series of chapbooks called Sprang Texts which presented the texts of local readings; and the suspension of Raddle Moon's "sidecar" publication, Giantess.

Another form of this essay, first given as a talk at the Kootenay School of Writing in Vancouver, May 2000, will appear as Documents in Poetics 2 (February, 2001).

She is the author of: Believing in the World: a reference work (Vancouver: Tsunami Editions, 1987); Suck Glow (forthcoming since 1989 from Leech Books, Vancouver); Mutability Lyrics (chapbook, in ms.); Theatre of the New World of the Time (book ms.); Tied to a post: an essay in abstraction; and Bad Infinity.

She has work in recent issues of The Gig, West Coast Line, and W, and work forthcoming on the Alterran Poetry Assemblage. She has made installations, a video collaboration, and a number of handmade book/installation pieces.

She works in Vancouver and electronically as a book editor, copy editor, indexer and typesetter.
