Poetic Economies of Performance

Biographies

 

Elizabeth-Jane Burnett is a poet and editor living and working in London. She studied English at Oxford, Poetic Practice (MA) at Royal Holloway, University of London, and Applied Poetics at Bowery Arts and Science, New York. Publications include This Actually Happened (Vox Pop, New York, 2005), Bowery Women: Poems (YBK Publishers, Inc. New York, 2006) and Liminal: Spaces-In-Between visible and invisible (Evolutionary Girls, New York, 2007). She also teaches Creative Writing and performs across the UK and US. She is interested in the possibilities for poetic economies, digital writing and performance, and is currently researching these areas on the PhD in Contemporary Poetics at Royal Holloway.

 

 

Kristin Prevallet was born in southwest Denver, CO. Daughter of Annie Prevallet, activist and nun; partial owner of Buckskin Joe's silver mine in Alma, CO; ancestry in Franche-Comte (Levier) France. Early studies in alchemy, mythology, and surrealism; recent studies in Olson, African poetry, modernism, Zen. Early education: Bluebirds (girl scouts), outward bound, Catholic Worker. Official education: got bad grades in high school but went on to study journalism at the University of Northern Colorado (Greeley); French literature at the Sorbonne (Paris, France); B.A. English, the University of Colorado (Boulder); M.A. Poetics and Media Study, the University of Buffalo. Now lives in Brooklyn with her daughter, Sophie. Books include: Scratch Sides: Poetry, Documentation and Image-text projects; SHADOW EVIDENCE INTELLIGENCE; I, Afterlife: Essay in Mourning Time; and most recently, she edited and introduced A Helen Adam Reader (National Poetry Foundation, 2007). She received a 2007 New York Foundation for the Arts Fellowship in Poetry.

 

 

Anne Waldman is a poet, editor, performer, professor, curator and cultural activist who carries in her genetics the lineages of the New American Poetry. Considered an inheritor of The Black Mountain, Beat (Allen Ginsberg called her his "spiritual wife") and New York School (Frank O'Hara told her to "work for inspiration, not money") mantles, she is author and editor of over 40 books and small press editions of poetry. The epic IOVIS project which she has been working on for over 25 years, is now running to 800 pages. Waldman has helped create and nurture poetry zones in the USA and abroad for much of her life. She ran the St. Mark’s Poetry Project in New York from 1966 - 1978, founded the Jack Kerouac School of Disembodied Poetics at Naropa with Allen Ginsberg, and the Study Abroad on the Bowery program in New York with Kristin Prevallet, Alan Gilbert and Bob Holman. She has received a National Endowment for the Arts award, the Shelley prize for poetry, and has had residences at the Civitella Ranieri Center in Umbria, The Atlantic Center for the Arts, The Rockefeller Foundation’s Bellagio Center and at the Christian Woman's University in Tokyo. She has taught experimental writing workshops at the Zen Mountain Monastery, as well as universities and colleges across the USA. She is co-founder of the Poetry Is News collective which curates forums of political and poetical discussion.  She makes her home in New York City and Boulder, Colorado and frequently travels to other poetry zones throughout the world, most recently the Conference on 20th Century American Poetry in Wuhan, China and at the International Literature Festival in Berlin (2007).
